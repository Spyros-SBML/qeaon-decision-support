Adverse outcome network · combined exposure
Each stressor (left) is wired through the IARC key characteristics it triggers — its molecular initiating / early key events — into the two mechanistic arms, initiation (mutation, genotoxic) and promotion (selection), converging on the adverse outcome in each target organ. Edge colour = mechanism; edge width = each stressor's contribution to the Absolute (IRIS-anchored) excess; highlighted nodes are shared across ≥2 stressors — the convergence that makes a mixture more than the sum of its parts. Toggle Graph/Flow for wiring vs. flux views.
Molecular AOP cascade · chemical-specific (AOP-Wiki)
The detailed, chemical-specific pathway from AOP-Wiki: each stressor's molecular initiating event → key events → the shared tissue key event clonal expansion of initiated cells (the q-eAON field φ) → the adverse outcome in its target organ. The cited AOP is shown under each chemical (TCDD AOP 41, benzo[a]pyrene AOP 397, BPA AOP 200, chloroform AOP 220; * = mechanistic proxy AOP 296 for arsenic/benzene, which have no stressor-linked cancer AOP). Colour = genotoxic (red) vs. non-genotoxic / receptor (blue); width = contribution to the Absolute excess; highlighted = key events shared across ≥2 chemicals — the mechanistic convergence. Edge opacity reflects evidence confidence; a dashed pink ⊣ modifier edge marks an interaction where one chemical alters another's key-event relationship (e.g. arsenic's DNA-repair inhibition amplifying dioxane-driven oxidative lesions → co-mutagenesis). Chemicals without a curated AOP fall back to the generic q-eAON cascade, or press ☍ Fill from AOP-Wiki to auto-pull a cited chain (Tier-0); curated Tier-1 chains take precedence.

Model definition & provenance
The initiated-field fraction φ evolves as dφ/dt = μ₀·m(t)·(1−φ) + s(t)·φ(1−φ) − ρ(t)·φ, with malignant hazard h(t)=c·φ·m(t)·gate(t) and life-course risk 1−exp(−∫h dt). Genotoxic stressor weight raises the mutation multiplier m(t); promoter weight raises the selection coefficient s(t) and establishment gate(t); repopulation ρ acts after exposure ceases (near-normal clones outcompete damaged field). The static AOP comparator freezes the selection layer (s=S₀, gate=1, ρ=0): the genotoxic-MIE toxicological reading. The TSCE limit is the constant-rate two-stage model (no exposome schedule, no repopulation) to which the eAON reduces — see manuscript §6.4. Defaults: μ₀, s, ρ anchored to Yoshida (2020) bronchial driver fractions; c, gate calibrated to never-/continuing-smoker lifetime risk (Peto 2000).
